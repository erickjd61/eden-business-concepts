We're glad to share that Heather Humphrey, Strategic Advisor at Eden Business Concepts, will join the plenary panel at [Gallagher's 2026 Communications Summit](https://events.ajg.com/event/comms-summit-chicago/Home) in Chicago on October 22, 2026.

Heather joins Ruby Thomas of JLL and Kristina Bracaglia of JPMorgan Chase for *The Clarity Factor: Developing Leadership Acumen and Agency in the AI Era* — a multigenerational conversation, Gen X to Gen Z, on the leadership skill that matters most as AI reshapes how teams work: clarity of judgment.

Their case is simple and bracing. Judgment is the product of two things — **acumen**, the ability to read context, history, and stakes, and **agency**, the willingness to take ownership and adapt to ambiguity. Communicators used to build it slowly, over years. Now even entry-level contributors supervise the output of AI, and they need that judgment far sooner. The panel offers a practical way to develop it: what they call *multidirectional stewardship* — leaders and team members actively giving and asking for the context and authority that real work requires.

It's a fitting room for Heather. For three decades she has helped leaders turn big ideas into real results — most recently as Director of Communications, Change & Culture at AbbVie — clarifying purpose first, then building the strategy, structure, and systems that carry the work forward. That's the same conviction that drives Eden: healthy, clear leadership is what lets good ideas actually land.

Congratulations, Heather. We'll be cheering you on.
